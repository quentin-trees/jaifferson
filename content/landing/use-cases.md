# Use cases — "Why this changes your life"

**Eyebrow**: USE CASES

**H2**: You already read the room. Now read it *before* you walk in.

## The interview
You're about to meet the person who decides your next five years. Fifteen minutes before the call, you know their cognitive style, what they actually value, and the one question that'll make them lean in. You walk in calibrated — not nervous.

## The first date
The bio gave you noise. The profile gives you signal. What they optimize for, what bores them, the topic that lights them up. You're not *strategizing* a date — you're just not wasting the first 45 minutes on small talk that misses.

## The sales meeting
Most reps pitch their product. You pitch into *their driver*. Same deck, different opening line — and suddenly you're not the 4th vendor that week, you're the one who got it.

## The investor pitch
You've raised once by guessing which partner cares about what. Now you know. Walk in with the *specific* objection they'll raise — already answered on slide 7.

## The senior hire
You're sizing up a VP candidate with a strong CV and a quiet gap between roles. The profile surfaces what they're actually optimizing for next — and whether that matches the job you're offering, or just the title.

## The board meeting
New board member joins next week. You used to wait three meetings to understand how they think. Now you know by Tuesday.

## The room you didn't ask for
The cocktail, the panel, the dinner seating. You look someone up on your phone under the table, and two minutes later you're having the conversation they didn't know they wanted.

---

**Underline**: *Not manipulation. Calibration. The same information they'd give you themselves — if you had three hours and they felt like talking.*
